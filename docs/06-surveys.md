# CoursePilot Survey

## 1. Introduction

A short student survey was designed as part of the requirement-gathering process for CoursePilot. The purpose of the survey was to collect quantitative feedback about common course-registration problems and determine which features students consider most important.

The survey focused on seat availability, waiting lists, prerequisite information, credit calculation, schedule conflicts, approval status, and final class schedules.

## 2. Survey Objectives

The survey was conducted to:

* Identify common course-registration difficulties.
* Understand whether students trust the displayed seat count.
* Measure student interest in a waiting-list system.
* Determine whether students want to see their waiting-list position.
* Understand the importance of prerequisite information.
* Determine whether schedule conflicts should block registration.
* Understand whether students need instant credit calculation.
* Identify the information students expect in their registered-course schedule.

## 3. Survey Method

The survey was created using Google Forms and shared with undergraduate students who had previous experience using a university course-registration portal.

## 4. Survey Questions

The survey contained the following questions:

1. What is your current academic level?
2. Have you experienced a course becoming full while completing registration?
3. Do you think the displayed seat count should update automatically?
4. Should students be able to join a waiting list when a course section is full?
5. Should students be able to see their current waiting-list position?
6. Should the system clearly show prerequisite courses before selection?
7. Should mandatory courses be clearly labelled?
8. Should the system show the total number of selected credits instantly?
9. Should the system block registration when two selected courses have overlapping schedules?
10. Should the system display the conflicting course name, section, day, and time?
11. Should rejected registration requests include a clear reason?
12. What information should appear in the final registered-course schedule?
13. Which CoursePilot feature would be most useful to you?

## 9. Requirements Identified from the Survey

The survey supports the following CoursePilot requirements:

1. The system should display automatically updated seat availability.
2. The system should verify seat availability again during confirmation.
3. Students should be able to join a waiting list when a section is full.
4. Students should be able to view their current waiting-list position.
5. Waiting-list order should follow a fair first-come, first-served process.
6. Prerequisite requirements should be visible before course selection.
7. Missing prerequisites should be clearly identified.
8. Mandatory courses should be clearly labelled.
9. The selected-credit total should update immediately.
10. Minimum and maximum credit limits should be validated.
11. Schedule conflicts should block course registration.
12. Conflict messages should display the affected course codes, sections, days, and times.
13. Rejected requests should include a clear reason.
14. Approved courses should appear in a complete schedule and weekly timetable.

## 10. Limitations

The survey has the following limitations:

* The number of participants was limited.
* Most participants were students rather than advisors or administrators.
* Responses reflect experience with one university registration system.
* Different departments may follow different registration procedures.
* The survey results may not represent the opinions of all university students.

## 11. Conclusion

The survey was designed to validate the problems identified through observation and student interviews.

The findings will be used to define CoursePilot's product scope, user stories, functional requirements, acceptance criteria, system design, database structure, and API endpoints.
