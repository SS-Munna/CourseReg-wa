# CoursePilot Information Gathering

## 1. Introduction

Information gathering is an important part of software requirements analysis. It helps identify the actual problems faced by users, understand existing processes, and determine the features that should be included in the proposed system.

For CoursePilot, information was gathered to understand the challenges students face during course registration and the responsibilities of academic advisors and administrators.

## 2. Objectives of Information Gathering

The main objectives were:

* To understand the current course-registration process.
* To identify common problems experienced by students.
* To understand how course approval is handled.
* To identify how seat capacity and waiting lists should work.
* To determine the academic rules related to prerequisites and credit limits.
* To understand how schedule conflicts should be handled.
* To identify the information students need after registration.
* To define the requirements of advisors and administrators.

## 3. Information Sources

The following sources were considered during requirement gathering:

### 3.1 Students

Students are the main users of the proposed system. Their feedback helps identify problems related to:

* Course selection
* Seat availability
* Waiting-list status
* Schedule conflicts
* Prerequisite requirements
* Registration approval
* Class schedule viewing

### 3.2 Academic Advisors

Academic advisors provide information about:

* Registration approval procedures
* Credit requirements
* Prerequisite validation
* Course-selection rules
* Reasons for registration rejection
* Student advising responsibilities

### 3.3 Department Administrators

Department administrators provide information about:

* Course creation
* Section management
* Seat limits
* Instructor assignment
* Room allocation
* Registration periods
* Waiting-list management

### 3.4 Existing Course-Registration Systems

Existing course-registration portals were reviewed to understand:

* Course-list presentation
* Registration-status display
* Seat availability
* Schedule information
* Approval workflow
* Common usability problems

### 3.5 University Registration Policies

General academic registration rules were considered, including:

* Prerequisite requirements
* Minimum credit limits
* Maximum credit limits
* Registration deadlines
* Course-capacity restrictions
* Advisor approval

## 4. Information-Gathering Methods

### 4.1 Observation

The existing course-registration process was observed to understand how students:

* Search for courses
* Select course sections
* Check schedules
* Submit registration requests
* Track registration status
* View final class schedules

Observation helped identify areas where students may experience confusion or delay.

### 4.2 Interviews

Informal interviews were planned with students and academic users.

The interviews focused on:

* Problems during course registration
* Difficulties caused by full course sections
* Need for waiting-list tracking
* Schedule-conflict handling
* Prerequisite validation
* Credit-limit checking
* Approval and rejection messages
* Student schedule presentation

### 4.3 Surveys

A short survey was designed to collect responses from students.

The survey included questions about:

* Course-seat visibility
* Waiting-list position
* Schedule-conflict detection
* Prerequisite checking
* Registration-status tracking
* Class-time and room information
* Overall registration experience

### 4.4 Document Review

Registration-related documents and general university policies were reviewed to understand:

* Academic rules
* Registration procedures
* Course eligibility
* Credit requirements
* Approval responsibilities

### 4.5 Comparative Analysis

Similar course-registration and enrollment systems were compared to identify useful features and common limitations.

The comparison focused on:

* User interface
* Course search
* Seat management
* Waiting-list support
* Registration workflow
* Error messages
* Schedule display

## 5. Key Findings

The information-gathering process identified the following important requirements:

1. Students should be able to view available courses and sections.
2. Every course section should have a fixed seat limit.
3. Students should see the number of available seats.
4. Students should be able to join a waiting list when a section is full.
5. Students should see their current waiting-list position.
6. The system should check prerequisites automatically.
7. The system should validate minimum and maximum credit limits.
8. The system should detect overlapping class schedules.
9. Final registration submission should be blocked when conflicts exist.
10. Students should be able to track pending, approved, rejected, and waitlisted statuses.
11. Rejected requests should include clear reasons.
12. Approved courses should appear in a student schedule.
13. The schedule should display course, section, instructor, time, room, and credit information.
14. Advisors should be able to approve or reject registration requests.
15. Administrators should be able to manage courses, sections, seats, schedules, rooms, and prerequisites.

## 6. Limitations

The information-gathering process may have some limitations:

* The number of interview and survey participants may be limited.
* Different universities may follow different registration rules.
* Some administrative requirements may change later.
* The initial project does not include full university ERP integration.
* Payment, grading, and attendance features are outside the current scope.

## 7. Conclusion

The information collected provides a clear understanding of the main problems in course registration and the needs of students, advisors, and administrators.

These findings will be used to prepare the Product Requirement Document, Software Requirements Specification, and Technical Design Document for CoursePilot.
