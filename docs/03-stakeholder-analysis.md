# CoursePilot Stakeholder Analysis

## 1. Introduction

Stakeholders are individuals or groups who are affected by the CoursePilot system or who influence its development, operation, and success.

CoursePilot involves several stakeholders, including students, academic advisors, department administrators, faculty members, and system administrators. Each stakeholder has different goals, responsibilities, concerns, and levels of influence over the system.

This document identifies the main CoursePilot stakeholders and analyzes their interests and expectations.

## 2. Stakeholder Identification

The major stakeholders of CoursePilot are:

1. Students
2. Academic advisors
3. Department administrators
4. Faculty members
5. System administrators
6. University management
7. Software development and maintenance team

## 3. Primary Stakeholders

### 3.1 Students

Students are the main users of CoursePilot.

#### Goals

Students want to:

* Log in securely.
* View available courses and sections.
* See available seat counts.
* Register for eligible courses.
* Join a waiting list when a section is full.
* View their waiting-list position.
* Check prerequisite requirements.
* Avoid schedule conflicts.
* Meet minimum and maximum credit requirements.
* Track registration status.
* View their approved class schedule.

#### Responsibilities

Students are responsible for:

* Providing valid login information.
* Selecting suitable course sections.
* Reviewing course prerequisites.
* Correcting schedule conflicts.
* Meeting credit requirements.
* Monitoring registration and waiting-list status.

#### Main Concerns

Students may be concerned about:

* Full course sections.
* Unclear waiting-list positions.
* Delayed registration approval.
* Missing prerequisite information.
* Schedule conflicts.
* Incorrect course or room information.
* Rejected registration requests without explanation.

#### Level of Influence

Students have high interest but medium influence. Their feedback is important because they are the primary users of the system.

---

### 3.2 Academic Advisors

Academic advisors review student registration requests and provide academic guidance.

#### Goals

Academic advisors want to:

* View student registration requests.
* Review selected courses.
* Check student credit loads.
* Confirm prerequisite completion.
* Approve or reject registration requests.
* Provide comments when necessary.
* Reduce manual checking and administrative work.

#### Responsibilities

Academic advisors are responsible for:

* Reviewing registration requests accurately.
* Approving valid course selections.
* Rejecting invalid requests with clear reasons.
* Ensuring that students follow academic rules.
* Advising students when changes are required.

#### Main Concerns

Academic advisors may be concerned about:

* Incorrect academic records.
* Excessive numbers of pending requests.
* Missing prerequisite data.
* Credit-limit violations.
* Schedule conflicts.
* Lack of clear decision history.

#### Level of Influence

Academic advisors have high interest and high influence because they directly control registration approval.

---

### 3.3 Department Administrators

Department administrators manage courses, sections, schedules, seats, rooms, and instructors.

#### Goals

Department administrators want to:

* Add and update courses.
* Create course sections.
* Set seat capacities.
* Assign instructors.
* Assign classrooms.
* Define course schedules.
* Define prerequisites.
* Monitor registrations and waiting lists.
* Open and close registration periods.

#### Responsibilities

Department administrators are responsible for:

* Maintaining accurate course information.
* Defining section capacity.
* Updating room and schedule information.
* Managing course availability.
* Monitoring waiting-list movement.
* Correcting administrative errors.

#### Main Concerns

Department administrators may be concerned about:

* Incorrect course data.
* Over-capacity enrollment.
* Room-capacity conflicts.
* Duplicate course sections.
* Incorrect prerequisites.
* Unfair waiting-list processing.

#### Level of Influence

Department administrators have high interest and high influence because they manage the academic data used by the system.

---

### 3.4 Faculty Members

Faculty members teach assigned course sections and may need access to course rosters.

#### Goals

Faculty members want to:

* View assigned courses and sections.
* View enrolled students.
* View class times and room numbers.
* Receive accurate course-roster information.

#### Responsibilities

Faculty members are responsible for:

* Confirming their assigned sections.
* Reporting incorrect course information.
* Reviewing section enrollment when necessary.

#### Main Concerns

Faculty members may be concerned about:

* Incorrect student rosters.
* Wrong room assignments.
* Incorrect class schedules.
* Enrollment above section capacity.

#### Level of Influence

Faculty members have medium interest and medium influence.

---

### 3.5 System Administrators

System administrators manage user accounts, permissions, security, and system configuration.

#### Goals

System administrators want to:

* Manage user accounts.
* Assign roles and permissions.
* Protect student and academic information.
* Maintain system availability.
* Monitor technical problems.
* Manage backups and system configuration.

#### Responsibilities

System administrators are responsible for:

* Creating and managing accounts.
* Maintaining role-based access.
* Protecting sensitive data.
* Monitoring system performance.
* Resolving technical issues.
* Maintaining backups and logs.

#### Main Concerns

System administrators may be concerned about:

* Unauthorized access.
* Data loss.
* Weak passwords.
* System downtime.
* Incorrect permissions.
* Security vulnerabilities.

#### Level of Influence

System administrators have high influence because they control technical access and system operation.

## 4. Secondary Stakeholders

### 4.1 University Management

University management may use reports and system information for academic planning.

#### Interests

* Efficient course-registration operations.
* Reduced administrative workload.
* Accurate enrollment information.
* Better student experience.
* Reliable academic data.

#### Level of Influence

University management has high influence but lower day-to-day involvement.

---

### 4.2 Software Development and Maintenance Team

The development team designs, implements, tests, and maintains CoursePilot.

#### Interests

* Clear requirements.
* Stable system architecture.
* Testable features.
* Secure implementation.
* Maintainable code.
* Successful deployment.

#### Responsibilities

* Develop frontend and backend components.
* Design the database.
* Implement business rules.
* Test system functionality.
* Fix defects.
* Maintain technical documentation.

#### Level of Influence

The development team has high influence during system development and maintenance.

| Academic advisors         | Registration review and approval            |      High |     High |
| Department administrators | Course, seat, room, and schedule management |      High |     High |
| Faculty members           | Accurate course and student information     |    Medium |   Medium |
| System administrators     | Security and system operation               |      High |     High |
| University management     | Efficient academic administration           |      High |   Medium |
| Development team          | Successful design and implementation        |      High |     High |

## 6. Stakeholder Communication Plan

| Stakeholder               | Information Needed                                  | Communication Method             |
| ------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------- |
| Students                  | Seats, status, waitlist position, schedule          | Student portal and notifications |
| Academic advisors         | Pending requests and academic validation            | Advisor dashboard                |
| Department administrators | Course, section, seat, and waiting-list information | Admin dashboard                  |
| Faculty members           | Assigned sections and class rosters                 | Faculty view or reports          |
| System administrators     | Logs, security alerts, and system health            | Administration panel             |
| University management     | Enrollment summaries and reports                    | Management reports               |
| Development team          | Requirements, issues, and technical tasks           | GitHub Issues and Project Board  |

## 7. Conclusion

CoursePilot must satisfy the needs of multiple stakeholders while keeping the student registration process simple and transparent.

Students require clear course, seat, waiting-list, and schedule information. Academic advisors need efficient approval tools. Department administrators need accurate course-management features, while system administrators require secure access control and reliable operation.

Understanding these stakeholder needs will help ensure that CoursePilot is useful, secure, and aligned with university registration procedures.
